Best Running Shoes for Overpronation

If the inner edges of your old shoes wear down fastest, or your ankles tend to roll inward as you run, you likely overpronate. It's extremely common and nothing to worry about — it just means you'll feel best in a stability shoe, which gently guides your foot into a straighter path instead of letting it collapse inward. Left unsupported, overpronation is a common driver of knee, shin, and hip aches that show up miles later, not at the moment. The shoes below all build in that guidance — through guiderails, medial posts, or wider, planted bases — while still feeling like a normal, comfortable trainer. They range from lighter, moderate-support options to plush, maximum-stability shoes, so there's a fit whether you're logging easy daily miles or standing all day.
