Best Running Shoes for Beginners

If you're just starting to run, the best shoe is a forgiving, do-everything daily trainer — not the flashiest, most cushioned, or fastest thing on the wall. You want something with a balanced amount of cushion (enough to be comfortable, not so much it feels unstable), a smooth ride, and a fit that works for a wide range of feet. These are the shoes that quietly handle your first weeks and months: easy runs, walk-run intervals, building up to your first 5K. Every pick below is a versatile, well-rounded trainer that's hard to go wrong with. Once you've got some miles in and know what you like, you can branch into softer or faster shoes — but this is where to begin.
