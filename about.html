About us

Two runners who got tired of guessing.

We're Ben and Billy, two lifelong runners who got tired of guessing.

Between the two of us, we've gone through more pairs of running shoes than we'd like to admit. Shoes that looked right on the wall and felt wrong by mile two. Shoes we bought because a friend swore by them, only to learn the hard way that someone else's perfect shoe can be the wrong shoe for a completely different foot. We've dealt with the blisters, the achy knees, the "maybe I just need to break them in more" denial, all of it. Finding the right shoe never felt as simple as it should be.

That's the whole reason this exists. We built The Shoe Recommender because we wished something like it existed for us, a way to skip the guesswork and the trial-and-error, and just get pointed toward the shoe that actually fits how you run.

We're not selling for anybody. No brand pays us, no warehouse needs clearing — our only job is figuring out what's actually right for your feet.

We don't work for a shoe brand, we don't have a warehouse of inventory to clear, and nobody's paying us to push their latest release. Our only job is figuring out what's actually right for your feet, even if that means recommending a shoe we don't make a dime from. If a $70 shoe is the right call over a $180 one, we'll tell you that.

We're still runners first. This is the tool we wanted when we were starting out, still lacing up for, and building for everyone else figuring out the same thing we were.

I got into it young and never really stopped, there's something about lacing up and getting out the door that's stuck with me through every phase of life since. I've dealt with my fair share of bad shoe decisions along the way, the kind where you don't realize something's wrong until your feet are telling you ten miles in. That's a big part of why this matters to me. I want people to skip the trial-and-error I went through and just get it right from the start.

The wrong pair doesn't just feel off, it can derail months of training. I've also spent plenty of time fitting people for shoes, and I've seen firsthand how much better people run, and how much less they hurt, once they're in something that actually fits them. That's exactly what we're trying to make easy here.
