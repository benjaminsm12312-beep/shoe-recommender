Best Running Shoes for a Wide Toe Box

Some runners never have quite enough room up in the front of their shoes. If your toes feel pressed or squeezed against the walls of the shoe, this list will show you three great options that tackle that exact problem.

Here's what many people get wrong: your toes are supposed to be free. They need space to spread on impact, not sit boxed in. A few brands build extra space in the toe box for this purpose — seen in Altra and Topo shoes.

Wide up front doesn't mean we want the shoe loose everywhere. The extra room should start and stop at the toes. You still want your heel and midfoot locked down so there's no slipping or rubbing, which can lead to discomfort or blisters. We're aiming for room to spread, not room to slide around.

Pro Tip

Room to spread, not room to slip

Toe box space matters more than people think, and letting your toes spread out is genuinely good for your feet. It's also important to know that the extra space should stop at the toes. Your heel and midfoot still need to be locked in so nothing slips around. The goal is enough room to spread out, not so much that you slide around in the shoe.
